\begin{abstract}
Reinforcement learning (RL) agents need to explore their environments in order to learn optimal policies by trial and error.
While it is currently typical to train RL agents mostly or entirely in simulation, where safety concerns are minimal.
However, it is very difficult to accurately describe complex real-world environments using simulations.
Therefore, efforts to train and validate in real-world environments are continuously ongoing.
Thus, research on constrained RL has emerged to ensure the stability of agents.
In this paper, we propose an algorithm that estimates the Lagrangian multiplier at each step to impose appropriate constraints on each state, ensuring stability.
\end{abstract}
